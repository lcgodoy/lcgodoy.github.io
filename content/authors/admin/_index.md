Hi, my name is Lucas. I'm from Brazil and currenlty I'm a PhD Candidate in
Statistics at UConn. 

I have chosen statistics when I was 18 years old, at that time I was working on
the 2010 Brazilian Census (at
[IBGE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazilian_Institute_of_Geography_and_Statistics))
as a supervisor. I was amazed about several things, more importantly how complex
and interesting was the process of collecting high quality data. When I realized
that the statisticians were the ones in charge of the planning of such a
intricate operationg, I made some research about the profession. Again, I was
astonished. This time it was not for the complexity of the tasks themselves, but
for how versatile statistics is, how helpful it is for so many (if not all)
fields of study (and for the society in general). 

These days, I'm everyday more and certain about the decision I have taken eleven
years ago. Gladly, I was right about the plurality of statistics as a field. As
a undergrad, I had the opportunity to work on projects on social epidemiology,
geosciences, and statistical consulting. As a master student, I worked with
environmental data, which was also very interesting. Besides statistics itself,
the most important projects that I worked in had something else in common:
spatial data, and consequently, the need of creating maps and taking in account
spatial dependence when making inference. Not surprinsingly, I'm very interested
in open data, spatial statiscis, and statistical computing. Although I have not
mentioned it yet, you might guess that I'm a huge fan of `R` and the `rstats`
community. This programming language (and its helpful comunity) has made
everything easier and smoother on my still ongoing journey on stats and data
analysis.

About the website, it was intended initially to exercise my writing in
English. As a PhD student it has been hard to find time for blogging but my
intention is to write here about interesting things (at least to me) whenever is
possible. Additionally, you can find here informatin about my Talks, ongoing
projects, and my CV.
